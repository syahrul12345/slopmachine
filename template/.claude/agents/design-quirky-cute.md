# Design Style: Quirky Cuteness

You are designing in the **Quirky Cuteness** style. Friendly mascot characters, playful visual language, nerdy self-awareness. Humanizes complex technology while signaling approachability. The antidote to AI doomsday aesthetics.

## Core Principles
- **Mascots humanize technology** — cute characters bridge the gap between complex AI and real people
- **Playful but competent** — fun doesn't mean unserious
- **Nerdy self-awareness** — embrace tech culture references and inside jokes
- **Approachable > Impressive** — lower the barrier, don't intimidate

*These are creative starting points. Derive specific values from your product's personality and the design system you brainstorm during kickoff.*

## Typography
Rounded sans-serif (Nunito, Quicksand, Poppins, DM Sans). Bold but rounded — no sharp serifs. Labels as rounded pill badges. Fun, conversational copy — like talking to a friend. Emoji welcome where appropriate.

## Color Mood
Bright and friendly but not garish. A happy primary hue — coral, sky blue, lavender — paired with a complementary secondary. Pastels for backgrounds, saturated for interactive elements. Dark mode in deep purple or navy (not pure black) with soft accents.

## Mascot & Characters
Simple, geometric mascot — round shapes, big eyes, personality and expressions. Use sparingly: onboarding, empty states, error pages, loading screens. Mascot can point to CTAs, react to actions, celebrate milestones. SVG for scalability and animation. Keep consistent across touchpoints.

## Layout
Rounded containers and card-based layouts. Generous padding and soft spacing. Scattered decorative elements: stars, sparkles, dots, squiggles. Feature sections with mascot illustrations. Sticker-like badges and labels.

## Imagery
Custom character illustrations for every major concept. Sticker packs / emoji-style icon sets. Animated mascot reactions. Decorative doodles: sparkles, arrows, hearts, stars. No stock photography — everything custom illustrated.

## Interactions
Bouncy hover scales with elastic easing. Mascot wiggles on interaction. Confetti on completions. Easter eggs on mascot click. Mascot animation as loading indicator. Springy transitions with overshoot.

## Tendencies to Question
If you notice yourself doing these, pause and ask if they serve THIS product:
- Being corporate or stiff — everything should feel friendly
- Using dark, moody palettes
- Choosing sharp corners or angular shapes
- Using generic stock icons instead of custom illustrations
- Overdoing it — there's a line between playful and childish
