# Design Style: Outer Space

You are designing in the **Outer Space** style. Cosmic imagery — galaxies, stars, celestial light. Combines romantic utopianism with exploration metaphors. AI as the final frontier.

## Core Principles
- **Exploration metaphor** — the product represents venturing into the unknown
- **Cosmic scale** — design communicates vastness and possibility
- **Romantic utopianism** — optimistic, aspirational, full of wonder
- **Celestial beauty** — stars, nebulae, and cosmic light as primary design elements

*These are creative starting points. Derive specific values from your product's personality and the design system you brainstorm during kickoff.*

## Typography
Modern geometric sans-serif. Light text on dark — white or soft blue-white. All-caps for navigation and labels. Generous letter-spacing on headlines for an expansive feel.

## Color Mood
Deep space: void blacks, deep navies. Stars in white and blue-white. Nebula accents: cosmic purple, nebula pink, star blue. Warm stars in gold and amber. Atmospheric glow — soft halos of color around light sources. High contrast between illuminated elements and the void.

## Techniques
- CSS star fields using layered radial gradients
- Nebula glow with radial gradients and low-opacity color
- Planet/sphere renders with radial gradients and layered box-shadows
- Lens flare effects with screen blend mode
- Canvas-based particle star fields for interactive density

## Layout
Full-dark backgrounds throughout — scrolling feels like traveling through space. Expansive, full-viewport hero with cosmic backdrop. Content sections float in space — translucent cards or no containers at all. Generous spacing. Wide layouts using the full viewport for the cosmic canvas.

## Imagery
Star fields (CSS or canvas), nebula gradients, cosmic dust. Planet/sphere renders with atmospheric glow. Orbital paths and trajectory lines. Constellation-style dot-and-line patterns. No earthbound imagery — stay cosmic.

## Interactions
Star parallax at multiple layers. Twinkling star brightness animation. Gravitational pull — elements subtly attract toward cursor. Warp speed effects on section transitions. Orbit animations. Occasional shooting stars.

## Tendencies to Question
If you notice yourself doing these, pause and ask if they serve THIS product:
- Using light or white backgrounds (breaks the space illusion)
- Letting warm/earthy colors dominate
- Building cramped, dense layouts (space needs space)
- Making it cliché — a space background alone isn't design, it needs depth and intention
